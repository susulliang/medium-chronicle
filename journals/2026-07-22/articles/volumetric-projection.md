The transition of real-time AI generation from digital displays into physical space is forcing a re-evaluation of projection art. Interactive installation design is increasingly utilizing spatial mapping, where continuous pipelines like StreamDiffusion are directed not at screens, but through volumetric fog and reactive particles.

By tying facial capture or motion tracking directly to high-lumen projectors, artists are creating environments where the latent space feels entirely tangible. These installations demand a highly optimized, low-latency loop, ensuring that the projection morphs instantly in response to human presence.

As documented in recent *Leonardo* journals, this represents a shift from observing a generated image to stepping *inside* its architecture. The volumetric projection becomes a live organism, continually reshaping its physical luminescence based on the data fed into its neural network.
