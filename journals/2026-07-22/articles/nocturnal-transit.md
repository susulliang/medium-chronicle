There is a unique psychology to viewing art during the overnight hours, particularly in spaces of transit or continuous operation. Recent installations have begun explicitly targeting this temporal displacement, deploying light and projection art meant to be experienced by those working overnight shifts or navigating international transit hubs.

These works differ drastically from daytime gallery pieces. They utilize slow, ambient projection mapping and soft, rhythmic physical lamps to mimic the strange, suspended reality of the late night. They do not demand immediate attention; rather, they provide a luminous companion to the isolated worker or traveler.

By bringing experimental light art out of the museum and into the 24-hour infrastructure of the city, artists are radically shifting who gets to interact with modern aesthetics, and under what psychological conditions.
