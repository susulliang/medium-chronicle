When preserving new media, curators often fixate on saving the software, but light-based installations face a much more tactile threat: hardware obsolescence. As specific models of halogen bulbs, custom LED controllers, and vintage 35mm projector lamps cease production, the original aesthetic of experimental light art is actively decaying.

The *Archive of Digital Art (ADA)* outlines the deep complexity of replacing physical lamps in historical installations. An artwork designed around the specific color temperature and flicker rate of a 1990s tungsten bulb is fundamentally altered when retrofitted with modern LEDs.

If the meaning of an installation relies on the physical constraints of its projection hardware, modern emulation and component swapping borders on revisionist history. The preservation of digital art must, paradoxically, include the hoarding of outdated glass and filament.
